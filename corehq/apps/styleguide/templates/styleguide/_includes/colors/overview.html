Color Overview

Colors serve to emphasize information and contribute to a more organic looking interface. The world is colorful place, so why shouldn't our software appear just as lively?

Keep in mind that emotional reactions to colors can vary geographically. For instance, red in Africa might mean something completely different from red in India. For this reason, it is important to use caution when choosing what function a color has in the interface, and expect the most negative reaction.

An additional point of consideration is that not everyone sees color the same way. Many people are affected by various types of color blindness. It is important to make sure that colors are never the source of critical information. Also, when colors are used to compare information (legend of a bar or line chart) it is important that the colors used can be differentiated by people with each type of color blindness.
